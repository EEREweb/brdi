Must Reads

  • Bioenergy Technologies Office Multi-Year Program Plan – March 2016 Update: This multi-year program plan (MYPP) sets forth the goals and structure of the Biomass Program at the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE). It identifies the research, development, demonstration, and deployment activities the Biomass Program will focus on over the next 5 years and outlines why these activities are important to meeting the energy and sustainability challenges facing the nation. The MYPP is intended for use as an operational guide to help the Biomass Program manage and coordinate its activities, as well as a resource to help articulate the program's mission and goals to management and the public.
  • Bioenergy Technologies Office Multi-Year Program Plan – March 2015 Update: This MYPP sets forth the goals and structure of the Biomass Program at DOE. It identifies the research, development, demonstration, and deployment activities the program will focus on over the next 5 years and outlines why these activities are important to meeting the energy and sustainability challenges facing the United States. The MYPP is intended for use as an operational guide to help the Biomass Program manage and coordinate its activities, as well as a resource to help articulate the program's mission and goals to management and the public.
  • U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Energy Brochure: USDA encourages and supports the development, production, and delivery of clean, renewable, domestically produced energy. USDA efforts cover the entire renewable energy supply chain: research and development activities; financial assistance to agriculture and forest producers for raising and harvesting energy crops; financing biorefineries that will produce renewable sources of fuel and power; and providing technical and financial assistance to agricultural producers and rural small businesses to assist them in becoming more energy efficient.
  • USDA Biofuels Production Roadmap: USDA developed a comprehensive regional strategy to help recharge the rural U.S. economy. The strategy targets barriers to the development of a successful biofuels market that will achieve, or surpass, the current U.S. Renewable Fuel Standard (RFS2), as set out in the Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007. USDA's objectives for this report include, providing the practical knowledge from the field that can enhance various models for biofuel production, identify challenges and opportunities, and help develop solutions to this massive undertaking.

Recommended Reads

  • The Renewable Fuel Standard: Issues for 2014 and Beyond: The Congressional Budget Office (CBO) released this report on the Renewable Fuels Standard. In this analysis, CBO evaluates how much the supply of various types of renewable fuels would have to increase over the next several years to comply with the Renewable Fuel Standard. CBO also examines how food prices, fuel prices, and emissions would vary in an illustrative year, 2017, under three scenarios for the Renewable Fuel Standard.
  • Renewable Chemicals and Materials Opportunity Assessment: The objective of this paper is to characterize the economic opportunities presented by this emerging industry in terms of job creation potential, investment requirements and value added over the next five to ten years. In addition, the obstacles to development are described and options are identified for derisking this emerging industry.
  • Biomass Research and Development Act of 2000 – Excerpt: The creation of the Biomass Board and TAC were initially mandated by the Biomass Research and Development Act of 2000.
  • U.S. Billion-Ton Update: The report, "Biomass as Feedstock for a Bioenergy and Bioproducts Industry: The Technical Feasibility of a Billion-Ton Annual Supply" (generally referred to as the "Billion-Ton Study" or "2005 BTS"), was an estimate of "potential" biomass within the contiguous United States based on numerous assumptions about current and future inventory and production capacity, availability, and technology. This report is an update to the 2005 BTS that addresses shortcomings and questions that arose from the original report.
  • Presidential Memorandum Establishing the Biofuels Interagency Working Group: President Obama established a Biofuels Interagency Working Group, to be co-chaired by the Secretaries of Agriculture and Energy and the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). This Working Group will work with the National Science and Technology Council's Biomass Research and Development Board in undertaking its work.
  • The Biofuels Interagency Working Group – Growing America's Fuels Report: The biofuel goals mandated by the president and Congress will be met by supporting the existing biofuel industry, while accelerating the commercial establishment of advanced biofuels, by increasing communication and having a strategic plan across the U.S. Government, and by employing strategic public-private partnerships. This plan builds upon the President's May 5, 2009, memorandum forming the Biofuels Interagency Working Group with high-level USDA, DOE, and EPA participation.
  • The National Research Council's Renewable Fuel Standard: Potential Economic and
    Environmental Effects of U.S. Biofuel Policy    : At the request of the U.S. Congress, the National Research Council convened a committee of 16 experts to provide an independent assessment of the economic and environmental benefits and concerns associated with achieving RFS2. The Committee drew on its own expertise and solicited input from many experts in federal agencies, academia, trade associations, stakeholders' groups, and nongovernmental organizations in a series of open meetings and in writing to fulfill the statement of task. For more information, view the presentation given to the Committee in November 2011.
  • Biomass R&D National Biofuels Action Plan (currently being updated): This Action Plan outlines areas where interagency cooperation will help evolve biobased fuel production technologies from promising ideas to competitive solutions. This document was developed through the efforts of the Biomass Research and Development Board.
  • Farm to Fly Resolution on Aviation Biofuels: USDA, Boeing, and the Air Transport Association of America, Inc., are working together to accelerate the availability of commercially viable and sustainable aviation biofuels in the United States to increase domestic energy security, establish regional supply chains, and support rural development. For more information, view the Farm to Fly Report.
  • Memorandum of Understanding with the Dairy Industry: DOE has teamed with the Innovation Center for U.S. Dairy. Their first priority is the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions. In 2009, they established a goal and roadmap for the entire value chain to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 25% by the year 2020. The team is also committed to accelerating and streamlining the process for adopting anaerobic digesters by U.S. dairy farm operators through various USDA programs.
  • Memorandum of Understanding with the Department of Navy: USDA and the Department of Navy are working jointly to encourage maximum use of renewable energy. The goal is to provide technical assistance and financial products to qualified entities for the development of advanced biofuels and other renewable energy systems to meet both commercial and military transportation needs.
  • Defense Production Act Memorandum of Understanding: DOE, USDA, and the Department of Navy were directed to work with private industry to create advanced drop-in biofuels that will power both the Department of Defense and private sector transportation throughout the United States. These departments will work in a cooperative effort to assist the development and support of a sustainable commercial biofuels industry and to foster cooperation to achieve the goals and objectives. A Request for Information was published to help the government further define its requirements.
  • Congressional Research Service (CRS) Reports: This link goes to a page containing several relevant CRS reports.

Additional Resources

  • Information about Biomass Program Peer Review: The Biomass Program conducted a detailed biennial peer review of its activities in 2011. The process included reviews of the Program's eight technology areas from February through April of 2011 and concluded in June 2011 with a two-day Program Peer Review Conference. The peer review process is requirement for all programs in DOE's Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy. Through the Peer Review, the Biomass Program obtains independent assessments of the current research and development project portfolio, providing an opportunity to obtain meaningful feedback from stakeholders. The information will be useful as the Program considers future funding and portfolio balance decisions. Final reports detailing the outcomes of the Platform and Program Peer Reviews can be found on the Biomass Program website.
  • USDA Energy Investments Maps: The Renewable Energy Investments Web map contains information regarding USDA programs that provide assistance to renewable energy and energy-efficiency projects.
