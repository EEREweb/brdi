Congressional Research Service Reports

The following recommended readings are all Congressional Research Service Reports related to biomass.

The Navy Biofuel Initiative Under the Defense Production Act, June 2012
The Secretaries of Energy, Agriculture, and Navy have entered into a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to assist the development and support of a sustainable commercial biofuels industry. This report discusses the latest developments in the MOU and funding from the Defense Production Act Initiative.

Biomass: Comparison of Definitions in Legislation through the 111th Congress, March 2012
The term biomass has been a part of legislation enacted by Congress for various programs over the past 30 years. This report investigates the characterization of biomass and the evolving definition of the term.

Biofuels Incentives: A Summary of Federal Programs, January 2012
This report outlines federal programs that provide direct or indirect incentives for biofuels. For each program described, the report provides details, which include administering agency, authorizing statute(s), annual funding, and expiration date.

Is Biopower Carbon Neutral?, July 2011
Congress has been increasingly interested in biopower—electricity generated from biomass feedstocks. This report analyzes the advantages of biopower and evaluates the premise that biopower is carbon neutral.

Biomass Feedstocks for Biopower: Background and Selected Issues, October 2010
Biopower is a form of renewable energy, and more specifically, the generation of electric power from biomass feedstocks. This report discusses the use of biomass for power generation, types of feedstocks, technical and economic advantages and challenges, and the amount of biopower feedstock available.

Renewable Fuel Standard (RFS): Overview and Issues, October 2010
This report summarizes the RFS mandate, as well as its implementation. It outlines some emerging issues related to the sustainability of the continued growth in U.S. biofuels production needed to fulfill the expanding RFS mandate, as well as the emergence of potential unintended consequences of this rapid expansion.
